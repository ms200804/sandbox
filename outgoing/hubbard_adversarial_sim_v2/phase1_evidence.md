# Evidence Specialist Analysis: Plaintiffs' Motion to Modify and Subordinate Attorney's Charging Lien

## Top 3 Weaknesses

1. **Blank declaration paragraphs throughout** — The Updated Schmidt Declaration is cited repeatedly with "¶ __" (blank paragraph numbers) for nearly every critical factual assertion: funding blockage, BF's refusal to produce records, bankruptcy details, Pravati's claims, self-funding costs, and the status of BF's firm entities. If these paragraphs are not filled in with specific, sworn testimony before filing, every factual assertion they support is attorney argument masquerading as evidence. — **Severity: fatal if unfiled; serious if merely unfiled at draft stage**

2. **No documentary evidence of BF's refusal to quantify** — The brief's central factual claim is that BF has "refused to quantify its quantum meruit claim" and "refused to produce time records" for two years. The only evidentiary support is a footnote in a prior opposition (Dkt. 327 at 10 n.5) adopted by the Court, and the forthcoming Updated Schmidt Declaration. There are no exhibits showing demand letters, emails requesting time records, or BF's refusals. For a two-year pattern of obstruction, the court should see the paper trail, not just counsel's summary of it. — **Severity: serious**

3. **Legalist's first-priority-lien requirement is unsupported by industry evidence** — The brief asserts that "litigation funders, as a standard industry practice, require a first-priority lien on any recovery" (Updated Schmidt Decl. ¶ __). This is a factual claim about industry custom that rests entirely on counsel's declaration. There is no declaration from Legalist, no term sheet, no correspondence confirming funding is available but for the lien, and no expert or industry affidavit on standard funder practices. An opposing party or skeptical judge could argue counsel is manufacturing urgency. — **Severity: serious**

## Strongest Single Concern

**The absence of a Legalist declaration or any funder documentation is the single biggest evidentiary gap.** The entire motion rests on the theory that the lien is blocking the case from proceeding. The mechanism is: lien → no funding → no co-counsel → case death. But the critical link — that Legalist is ready, willing, and able to fund *but for* the lien — is supported only by counsel's own say-so. A one-paragraph declaration from a Legalist representative, or even a redacted term sheet or engagement letter with a condition precedent referencing lien resolution, would convert the motion's central factual premise from attorney argument into admissible evidence. Without it, BF's opposition will argue this is a self-serving narrative unsupported by competent evidence from the actual decision-maker (the funder).

## Full Analysis

### Well-Supported Assertions

**The Court's prior ruling (Dkt. 329 adopting Dkt. 327) is well-documented and properly cited.** The brief correctly relies on the docket record for the proposition that BF is limited to quantum meruit. This is law of the case and needs no additional evidentiary support beyond the docket entries themselves. Section II is well-constructed on this point and I have no material concerns about the evidentiary foundation for the legal-framework argument.

**The lien's facial content (Dkt. 284) is a matter of record.** The assertion that the lien claims "at least 37.5% of any sum recovered" is verifiable from the docket. No evidentiary issue here.

**Co-counsel withdrawal (Dkt. 431) is a matter of record.** Properly documented.

**The Nematzadeh Declaration (¶¶ 2, 4) appears to exist and be cited with specificity.** This is the one third-party declaration actually referenced with filled-in paragraph numbers, suggesting it has been drafted. This supports the availability of replacement co-counsel.

**The bankruptcy filing is a matter of public record.** The case number (*In re John G. Balestriere*, No. 1:24-bk-11422) is cited. Bankruptcy docket entries are judicially noticeable under FRE 201. However — see below regarding the trustee abandonment.

### Assertions Needing Stronger Evidentiary Support

**1. Trustee abandonment of BF entities.**
The brief states the trustee "abandoned BF's firm entities ... as having no viable assets." The sole citation is "Updated Schmidt Decl. ¶ __." Counsel's declaration is not the best evidence of a bankruptcy trustee's actions. The abandonment should be documented by:
- The bankruptcy docket entry showing the motion to abandon or notice of abandonment
- Or a declaration/letter from the trustee (Albert Togut) confirming abandonment

The trustee's actions are provable from the bankruptcy docket and should be offered as an exhibit or at minimum be the subject of a request for judicial notice. Relying on counsel's characterization invites a hearsay objection and a best-evidence challenge.

**2. Pravati Capital's claims and conduct.**
The brief asserts Pravati is a creditor "with claims reportedly approaching $20 million" and has "asserted its own lien against BF" and "separately claimed entitlement to a percentage of any recovery in this case." Each assertion rests on "Updated Schmidt Decl. ¶ __." The word "reportedly" is a red flag — it signals that counsel is reporting secondhand information. These are factual assertions about a third party's legal claims. Supporting evidence should include:
- Pravati's proof of claim from the bankruptcy docket (judicially noticeable)
- Any correspondence from Pravati asserting rights to a percentage of recovery in *this* case (exhibit to declaration)

Without documentation, these assertions risk being characterized as speculation or inadmissible hearsay.

**3. BF's subordination demands.**
The brief states BF's principal "would not agree to subordination unless Plaintiffs agreed to pay BF a percentage of any recovery and release all claims against BF" (Hubbard Decl. ¶ 5 (Dkt. 327-2)). This is cited to the *original* Hubbard Declaration from 2024. Two concerns:
- Was this statement based on personal knowledge or relayed through counsel? If BF's principal communicated this demand to *counsel* and counsel told Hubbard, it's double hearsay.
- This is from March 2024 — two years ago. Has there been any subsequent communication? The brief implies BF's position is unchanged but cites no post-2024 evidence of continued refusal.

**4. The $20,000 in discovery platform costs.**
Cited to "Updated Schmidt Decl. ¶ __." This is provable if counsel attaches invoices or account statements. Standing alone, a round number in a declaration without documentation looks like an estimate rather than a verified figure. If the exact amount matters (and it does — it supports the "solo practitioner self-funding" narrative), attach the receipts.

**5. "Communications with BF's bankruptcy trustee suggest that contemporaneous billing records may not exist."**
This is vague, hedged, and potentially inadmissible. "Suggest" and "may not exist" is not testimony that records don't exist. Who communicated with the trustee? What was said? Is this counsel's characterization of a phone call? If so, opposing counsel will argue it's hearsay. If the trustee said records don't exist, get a declaration or letter from the trustee saying so. If the trustee said they were abandoned with other assets, say that precisely.

### Structural/Presentation Evidentiary Issues

**The blank paragraph citations ("¶ __") are pervasive.** I count approximately 12-15 blank paragraph references to the Updated Schmidt Declaration. This suggests the declaration has not been finalized. Every one of these is a load-bearing factual assertion. If even one paragraph is omitted or poorly drafted in the final declaration, the corresponding argument in the brief loses its foundation.

**No exhibit list is referenced or attached.** For a motion of this consequence, I would expect:
- Exhibit A: BF's Notice of Charging Lien (Dkt. 284)
- Exhibit B: Relevant correspondence showing demands to produce time records and BF's refusals
- Exhibit C: Legalist correspondence or term sheet (redacted as needed)
- Exhibit D: Bankruptcy docket entries showing filing and abandonment
- Exhibit E: Any Pravati correspondence asserting claims

The brief makes factual assertions that demand documentary support. Declarations summarizing documents are weaker than the documents themselves.

**The brief conflates "attorney argument" with "evidence" in several places.** For example: "The lien is the sole obstacle" is stated as fact but is really a conclusion drawn from multiple premises, each of which needs independent evidentiary support. The chain is: (1) Legalist requires first-priority lien → (2) BF's lien is in first position → (3) BF won't subordinate → (4) therefore no funding → (5) therefore no co-counsel → (6) therefore case can't proceed. Links 1, 3, 4, and 5 all need third-party evidence, not just counsel's declaration.

### What Is Sound

**The legal arguments in Sections I and II are well-supported by the cited authorities and the existing record.** The Court's own prior ruling is the strongest piece of evidence in the brief, and the brief deploys it effectively. The argument that the lien is facially inconsistent with the Court's framework requires no evidence beyond the docket — the lien says 37.5%, the Court said quantum meruit, and these are irreconcilable on the face of the documents.

**Section III's proposed remedy structure is legally sound and does not require additional evidentiary support** — it is a request for prospective relief (modify, substantiate, subordinate) rather than a factual assertion.

### Recommendations

1. **File declarations from Legalist and/or Nematzadeh confirming the lien is the blocking condition.** The Nematzadeh Declaration appears to exist but should explicitly state that the lien is the reason engagement is contingent. A Legalist declaration — even two paragraphs — transforms the motion.

2. **Attach correspondence as exhibits.** The two-year refusal to produce records should be documented with actual emails or letters, not just counsel's summary.

3. **Get a trustee declaration or cite bankruptcy docket entries directly** for the abandonment and the absence of records.

4. **Fill in every "¶ __" with precise, sworn testimony** and ensure each assertion rests on personal knowledge, not information and belief (except where FRCivP 11 permits it for matters not within declarant's personal knowledge on a motion).

5. **Replace "reportedly" and "suggest" with definite statements or drop the assertions.** Hedged language in a brief signals to the court that counsel isn't confident in the evidentiary basis.
