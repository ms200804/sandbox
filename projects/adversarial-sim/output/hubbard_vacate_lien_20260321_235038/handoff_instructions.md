## Key takeaways for the revision

### Must-do before filing
- **Finalize the Updated Schmidt Declaration** — fill all blank ¶ numbers, attach exhibits. This is the single fatal weakness; 80% of the factual record depends on it.
- **Get a Legalist declaration** — 1-2 paragraphs confirming funding is blocked by the lien. Currently hearsay through counsel's characterization.
- **Get the Nematzadeh declaration finalized** with specific paragraph references.
- **Pull and attach bankruptcy docket entries** — abandonment order, Pravati proof of claim, case status. These are public PACER records.

### Structural changes
- **Reorder the brief**: Lead with obstruction/stakes (current Section II), then facial defectiveness reframed (current Section I), then modification/subordination as PRIMARY relief (current Section III elevated). Vacatur becomes the enforcement mechanism if BF doesn't comply with modification.
- **Add a section on Magistrate Judge authority** under § 636(b)(1)(A). Four agents independently flagged this gap. Frame modification/subordination as non-dispositive docket management.
- **Add a due process paragraph** — serve on BF's principal, trustee, and Pravati; request a hearing; frame relief as preserving BF's quantum meruit claim, not extinguishing it.

### Language changes
- Replace all "the Court has determined" / "law of the case" language with "the Court has applied a legal framework under which" — Dkt. 329 denied intervention, it didn't adjudicate lien rights.
- Add a choice-of-law / judicial estoppel paragraph — BF invoked § 475-a in its own lien notice, can't pivot to Texas law.
- Cut the White Lilly footnote entirely. The bankruptcy and obstruction speak for themselves.
- Reduce the for-cause reservation (Section IV) to a single sentence.

### Research needed
- Fifth Circuit / WDTx authority on pre-judgment lien management or modification. Even unpublished opinions would help. This is the biggest doctrinal gap.
- Verify the *Thelen* citation supports the proposition as used.
- Check Magistrate Judge Chestney's standing orders for local requirements.

### Strategic decisions (for Matt)
- Consider proactively inviting BF to appear at the hearing rather than waiting for them to object. Defuses the due process argument.
- Assess the Pravati risk — pull the proof of claim from the bankruptcy docket. If Pravati has a direct contractual claim on this case's recovery (not just a claim against BF), that needs to be addressed.
- File a companion motion for expedited consideration tied to the July 23 discovery deadline.
