# Evidence Specialist Analysis: Hubbard v. Crow — Motion to Vacate Charging Lien

## Top 3 Weaknesses

1. **The "Updated Schmidt Declaration" is a ghost document** — The brief cites "Updated Schmidt Decl. ¶ __" approximately 15 times with blank paragraph numbers. This is not a formatting placeholder problem — it means the motion's factual backbone rests on a declaration that either doesn't exist yet or hasn't been finalized. Every factual assertion supported solely by this declaration is currently *unsupported attorney argument*. If this motion is filed without that declaration, BF can strike virtually every factual claim in the Statement of Facts. — **Severity: fatal**

2. **No documentary evidence of BF's refusal to quantify or subordinate** — The brief's central narrative is that BF stonewalled for two years. But the only evidence cited for BF's refusal to subordinate is Hubbard Decl. ¶ 5 (a client's recounting of what BF's principal "stated") and the Updated Schmidt Declaration (which doesn't exist yet). There are no attached emails, letters, or correspondence showing the demands, the refusals, or the two years of requests for time records. The footnote in Dkt. 327 at 10 n.5 is referenced, but that was adopted in a different procedural context (intervention). For this motion, the court needs fresh, direct evidence of the obstruction. — **Severity: serious**

3. **Bankruptcy and abandonment facts are unsupported by court records** — The brief asserts the trustee "abandoned" BF's firm entities, that Pravati Capital has claims "reportedly approaching $20 million," and that Pravati has "asserted its own lien" against BF's interest in this case. Every one of these assertions cites only the Updated Schmidt Declaration. None cite bankruptcy docket entries, trustee filings, or Pravati's own filings. The word "reportedly" is a red flag — it signals counsel doesn't have the underlying document. These are publicly available bankruptcy records; they should be exhibits. — **Severity: serious**

## Strongest Single Concern

**The Updated Schmidt Declaration does not exist as a finalized, executed document, and the motion cannot be filed in this state.** Roughly 80% of the factual assertions in the Statement of Facts — funding blockage, self-funding costs, BF's refusal to produce records, bankruptcy details, Pravati's claims, the availability of replacement counsel and funder — are supported *only* by this declaration with blank paragraph references. If BF opposes and challenges the factual record, the court has almost nothing to rely on beyond the prior docket (Dkt. 327, 327-1, 327-2) and the Nematzadeh Declaration. The brief reads as if the facts are established, but the evidentiary foundation is a placeholder.

## Full Analysis

### What Is Well-Supported

**The prior court rulings.** The brief's strongest evidentiary foundation is the existing docket. The Court's order at Dkt. 329 adopting Dkt. 327 is a matter of record. The charging lien at Dkt. 284 speaks for itself — it does claim 37.5%. The withdrawal of co-counsel at Dkt. 431 is on the docket. The scheduling order at Dkt. 436 is on the docket. These facts need no declaration. The argument in Section I (lien is facially inconsistent with the court's ruling) is the strongest section precisely because it relies on documents already in the record rather than new evidence.

**The Nematzadeh Declaration.** This apparently exists as a separate document with specific paragraph citations (¶¶ 2, 4). It establishes the availability of replacement co-counsel. This is narrow but real.

**The original Hubbard and Schmidt Declarations.** The brief cites Dkt. 327-1 (Schmidt Decl.) and Dkt. 327-2 (Hubbard Decl.) from the intervention opposition. These are already on the docket. However, they were filed nearly two years ago (April 2024). The court may want updated testimony, not just updated citations.

### What Needs More Evidence

**1. The $20,000 self-funding claim.** The brief states counsel has absorbed "approximately $20,000 in discovery platform costs alone." This is cited only to the Updated Schmidt Declaration. This is a concrete, verifiable number — it should be supported by invoices, receipts, or platform billing statements attached as exhibits. Without them, it's just counsel's say-so about his own expenditures.

**2. The funding blockage.** The brief claims Legalist requires a first-priority lien and cannot proceed while BF's lien remains. This is the linchpin of the prejudice argument. It is supported by:
- Schmidt Decl. ¶ 7 (from 2024) — good, but stale
- Updated Schmidt Decl. ¶ __ — doesn't exist yet

There should be a letter or email from Legalist confirming: (a) they are prepared to fund; (b) they require first-priority; (c) BF's lien is the specific obstacle. Ideally, a declaration from a Legalist representative. Opposing counsel will argue that counsel's characterization of a funder's requirements is hearsay. A direct communication from Legalist, offered not for truth but to show the effect on the listener (counsel's understanding of funder requirements), helps — but a declaration is cleaner.

**3. BF's refusal to produce time records.** The brief says "repeated requests over two years" were made. Where are they? Attach the emails or letters. Attach BF's responses (or show the absence of response). The brief cites Dkt. 327 at 10 n.5, but that's a footnote in a prior brief, not evidence. The court adopted the *legal analysis* in Dkt. 327, not necessarily every factual assertion in its footnotes.

**4. BF's subordination demands.** Hubbard Decl. ¶ 5 states what BF's principal "stated." This is a client's recollection of a conversation — likely hearsay if offered for the truth of BF's position. If it was in writing (email, letter), attach it. If it was oral and only Hubbard heard it, explain the circumstances. If counsel was present, counsel's declaration is better (attorney-to-attorney communication, less likely to be challenged on accuracy).

**5. The bankruptcy facts.** These are all verifiable from PACER:
- Filing date, Chapter 7 status — cite the docket entry
- Trustee's abandonment of firm entities — cite the specific abandonment order or report
- Pravati's claims — cite the proof of claim filing, with exhibit
- "No assets" — cite the schedules

None of this should rest on a declaration. Attach the bankruptcy filings as exhibits. The brief currently says "reportedly approaching $20 million" — this hedging language signals you don't have the document. Get it.

**6. Pravati's independent claim.** The assertion that Pravati has "claimed entitlement to a percentage of any recovery in this case independent of BF's interest" is a significant factual claim. If Pravati has made this claim in writing, attach it. If it was made in the bankruptcy proceeding, cite the filing. If it's based on counsel's understanding from conversations, that's hearsay and speculation. This fact, if true, strengthens the argument considerably (it shows the lien proceeds may not even reach BF), but it must be documented.

**7. The White Lilly arbitration.** The footnote citing *White Lilly, LLC v. Balestriere PLLC* and the AAA arbitration finding is powerful but sits in a footnote with only a "slip op." citation. Attach the arbitration award and the district court confirmation order as exhibits. BF will object that this is character evidence under FRE 404(b). Anticipate that — the relevance isn't propensity but rather the equitable analysis under *Schneider* (whether attorney conduct warrants forfeiture or limitation of lien rights).

### What Is Currently Just Attorney Argument

- "The lien is the sole obstacle" — stated repeatedly but supported only by counsel's characterization. Needs funder and co-counsel declarations.
- "Contemporaneous billing records may not exist" — cite: Updated Schmidt Decl., which attributes this to "communications with BF's bankruptcy trustee." This is double hearsay (counsel recounting what the trustee told him about BF's records). Get a declaration from the trustee, or at minimum attach the correspondence.
- "Attorney fee awards have exceeded the underlying verdict" in related BF litigation — cite: Updated Schmidt Decl. No case citation, no docket number, no exhibit. This is a throwaway assertion without support.
- The characterization that BF's conduct is "obstructive rather than protective" — this is legal argument dressed as fact. That's fine for the Argument section, but the underlying facts must be documented.

### Authentication and Hearsay Issues

| Evidence | Issue | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Hubbard Decl. ¶ 5 (BF's subordination demands) | Hearsay if offered for truth of BF's position | Reframe as effect on listener, or produce the written communication |
| "Communications with BF's bankruptcy trustee" | Double hearsay (counsel → trustee → BF's records) | Trustee declaration or attach correspondence |
| Pravati's claims "reportedly approaching $20M" | Hearsay, unverified | Attach proof of claim from bankruptcy docket |
| "Legalist has confirmed this requirement repeatedly" | Hearsay unless Legalist declares | Legalist representative declaration |
| White Lilly arbitration findings | Potentially inadmissible character evidence; also needs authentication | Attach certified copies; brief admissibility under equity/lien forfeiture framework |

### Bottom Line

Section I of the brief is strong because it relies on the existing record — the lien says 37.5%, the court already ruled that's impermissible, therefore the lien is facially defective. That argument needs almost no additional evidence.

Sections II and III depend entirely on evidence that is either stale (2024 declarations), placeholder (Updated Schmidt Decl. ¶ __), or missing (funder declaration, correspondence showing refusal, bankruptcy exhibits). The narrative is compelling but currently unsupported. Before filing:

1. **Finalize the Updated Schmidt Declaration** with specific paragraph numbers and attach all referenced exhibits.
2. **Obtain a declaration from Legalist** (or at minimum a letter) confirming the funding is blocked by the lien.
3. **Attach bankruptcy docket entries** — don't characterize them through counsel's declaration.
4. **Produce the correspondence** showing BF's refusal to quantify or subordinate.
5. **Get the Nematzadeh Declaration finalized** (it appears to exist but confirm).
6. **Attach the White Lilly materials** if you're going to cite them.

The motion's legal theory is sound. The evidentiary record is not yet built to support it.
