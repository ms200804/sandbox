# Example Scenario: Motion to Compel Arbitration

## Forum
SDNY (Southern District of New York)

## Position
Advocate represents **Plaintiff** opposing a motion to compel arbitration.

## Context
Plaintiff is a minority shareholder in a Delaware LLC. The operating agreement contains a broad arbitration clause requiring "all disputes arising under or relating to this Agreement" to be submitted to JAMS arbitration. Plaintiff brings claims for breach of fiduciary duty, fraudulent inducement, and unjust enrichment. Plaintiff argues:

1. The fiduciary duty claims arise under Delaware common law, not "under" the agreement
2. The fraudulent inducement claim challenges the formation of the agreement itself, making the arbitration clause unenforceable as to that claim
3. The JAMS clause is unconscionable because JAMS fees are prohibitively expensive relative to the amount in controversy

## Adversary Calibration
Aggressive (BigLaw defense counsel)

## Key Authorities (Starting Point)
- *Buckeye Check Cashing v. Cardegna*, 546 U.S. 440 (2006) — separability doctrine
- *Rent-A-Center v. Jackson*, 561 U.S. 63 (2010) — delegation clauses
- *Coinbase v. Bielski*, 599 U.S. 736 (2023) — interlocutory appeal stays
- *In re: Salomon Inc. S'holders' Deriv. Litig.*, 68 F.3d 554 (2d Cir. 1995)
